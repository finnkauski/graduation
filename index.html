What next?

Find small projects!

  • Find small things in your immediate workspace and personal that you could automate!

  • Scout out areas in your department that you could help with

Someone struggling to make charts and production reports each month?

Someone needs to replicate an old system in a reproducible and flexible way?

But it's not DataScience‚Ñ¢!

Maybe not, but if people are worried about these things they won't ask the deeper questions about their data which is where all the fancy machine learning could come in.

Take advantage of stuff you can practice on! It's both business relevant and good practice!

In the end you have to all this stuff in data science projects anyway. Might as well practice!

You forge how others perceive you!

`oh you're the python/R person within my immediate surroundings and you've shown you can solve my problems`

Once that is true, work will come and it will become a self reinforcing cycle!

Keep learning!

  • But if you do want to learn and continue down the rabbithole, ask for development time!

  • Don't get distracted (maybe try pomodoro, google it)

  • And make sure to find an outlet where to apply these skills

Imagine a world without failure

I worked in Prices division of ONS

The Consumer Prices Index is non-revisable

That is a situation where we CAN NOT fail

Imagine a world without failure

As soon as you come around with any new idea you get questions about how reliable it is

It becomes hard to push through anything experimental

But not impossible

Imagine a world without failure

Change will be incremental, but you have to once again show how useful the things you learn are

And more importantly you need to establish a culture of acceptance surrounding unsuccessful projects
